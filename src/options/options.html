CC Search Browser Extension

This extension providers a new interface to CC Search to help you search for free content in the public domain and under Creative Commons licenses.

This project is Open-Source. Your participation in the form of bug reports, feature requests or code contributions is very much appreciated.

What is the sync feature of the extension?

Chrome and Firefox have a built-in feature that syncs browser settings and preferences across your logged-in devices. The extension leverages this feature to sync your custom settings and bookmarks. This guarantees that the content you have bookmarked on your work computer will be present on your personal computer.

How do I activate the sync feature in Google Chrome?
  • Open the Chrome browser.
  • Paste "chrome://settings/syncSetup" in the address bar and press enter.
  • Turn on sync by pressing the "Turn on" button.
  • Click on "Manage sync".
  • Toggle on the "Extensions" option if not already turned on.
How do I activate the sync feature in Mozilla Firefox?
  • Open the Firefox browser.
  • Click the Firefox Account icon in the toolbar.
  • Click "Sign in to Firefox".
  • After signing in, click on the Firefox Account icon again and click on "Sync Settings".
  • You will see the items you are syncing. If "Add-ons" is not present in the list, click on the "Change" button and select it.
How do I update the extension?

We constantly push new versions of the extension with bug fixes and new features. By default, the extension is updated to the new version automatically by your browser, but this may not happen immediately. To update the extension manually you can perform the following steps:

Google Chrome

  • Open the Chrome browser.
  • In the address bar, type "chrome://extensions" and press enter.
  • At the top of the extensions page, toggle on the "Developer mode" option.
  • Press the "Update" button

Mozilla Firefox

  • Open the Firefox browser.
  • In the address bar, type "about:addons" and press enter.
  • Click the gear icon in the upper-right area of the Extensions panel.
  • Click the "Check for updates" option.

Opera

  • Open the Opera browser.
  • Click on menu > Extensions > Extensions (or press Ctrl + Shift + E)
  • At the top of the extensions page, toggle on the "Developers mode" option.
  • Press the "Update" button.

When you search, you can enter special symbols or words to your search term to make your search results more precise.

Search for an exact match

Put a word or phrase inside quotes. For example, "Claude Monet".

Search for multiple terms

Put '+' between the terms that you want to search for. For example, india +"taj mahal" will return results that contain both "taj mahal" and "india".

Exclude terms

Put '-' before the term that you'd like excluded from the results. For example, india -"taj mahal" will return results that contain "india" and not "taj mahal".

Search by title

Put title: in front of your search term to see only results whose title contains the specified. For example, title:"cats".

Search by creator

Put creator: in front of your search term to see only results from that creator. For example, creator:"Bernard van Orley".

Search by license

Put license: in front of your search term to see only results from the specified license. For example, license:"by".

The results will be images licensed under the CC-BY license. You can use any of the other CC licenses: "by", "by-sa", "by-nc", "by-nd", "by-nc-sa", "by-nc-nd", "cc0", "pdm".

Search by provider

Put provider: in front of your search term to see only results from the specified source. For example, provider:"met".

Note that you must use one of the provider codes specified below, searching for something like provider:"Metropolitan Museum of Art" will not return any results.

Search by tags

Put tags.name: in front of your search term to see only results that are tagged with the specified tag. For example, tags.name:"sky".

If you want to combine tags, you can use the AND operator, or the OR operator, depending on your search preference. For example:

  • tags.name:"sky" AND tags.name:"airplane". This will search for images with both tags, sky and airplane.
  • tags.name:"phone" OR tags.name:"sky". This will search for images tagged with sky or with phone, but not necessarily both.

Combining keywords

Put AND in between two queries to see only results that match both queries. For example:

  • title:"nature" AND provider:"met" will return images from the MET and whose title contains nature.
  • tags.name:"sky" AND tags.name:"airplane" will return images tagged both sky and airplane.

Put OR in between two queries to see only results that match either query. For example:

  • provider:"met" OR provider:"flickr" will return images which are either from MET or from Flickr.
  • tags.name:"phone" OR tags.name:"tablet" will return images tagged either phone or tablet.

Put NOT in between two queries to see only results that match the first query and do not match the second query. You can also use parentheses to combine more than one of these operations For example:

  • license:"by" AND (NOT provider:"flickr") will return images licensed under CC BY from all providers except from Flickr.
  • tags.name:"bird" AND (NOT tags.name:"flamingo") will return all images tagged bird except images also tagged flamingo.

Boosting a term

To boost a term use the caret, "^", symbol with a numberat the end of the term. The higher the number, the higher priority the term will be given in the search.

For example, dogs huskies^4, will make results containing "huskies" appear higher in the search results.
